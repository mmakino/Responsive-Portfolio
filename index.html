About Me

I have been an engineer in semiconductor indutry for about 20 years. The companies I have worked for provide yield management services for semiconductor manufacturing companies worldwide, based on complex systems of wafer testing equipments, and proprietary software systems for analyzing the data. I develop whatever software tools needed to connect and integrate such different components as IC design, inspection data, and analysis software. The two most interesing aspects I love are, communication and problem sovling. I constantly communicate with different groups of people such as IC layout designers, equipment application engineers, and data analysts and consultants to identify, understand, and troubleshoot whatever issues we have. In order to make the system of different components work as a whole, I use my knowledge and skills to implement solutions in software tools.


Born to parents of social science professors in Japan, I grew up wanting to be a philosopher (seriously!) as a child. Interestingly, I went to the University in Reno, Neveda, where I have my B.A. in Sociology (why I ended up in Reno all the way from Japan is another story). When I crashed into a crummy dorm room, I encountered a roommate in Computer Science major. Since then my life started changing dramatically. Although I attempted to get a degree in CS as well, I left there with minor in CS when I had a job offer for an application engineer.
